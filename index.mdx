## What is IncoPay?

**IncoPay** is **private, session-based x402 payments on Solana**: a user signs **once** to open a session with a spending cap and expiry, then the facilitator streams confidential per-call settlements on their behalf — no extra wallet popups, no gas. It is powered by [Inco Network](https://inco.org) and the **Inco Lightning** program for encrypted amounts and confidential transfers.

The session scheme is shipped as the [`solana-x402-sessions`](https://www.npmjs.com/package/solana-x402-sessions) SDK, with a hosted facilitator running on Solana devnet at [`inco-facilitator-production.up.railway.app`](https://inco-facilitator-production.up.railway.app). The facilitator pays the SOL fee on every settle from its own funded keypair, so end users are **gasless** end-to-end. A classic single-payment x402 flow is also exposed under `/pay/*` for one-shot demos.

On-chain, the session lifecycle leans on **IncoToken** (`approve` allowance + `transfer_with_authorization`) and **Inco Lightning** (FHE ciphertext arithmetic in a TEE). Total cap and expiry are enforced on-chain by the IncoToken allowance PDA; per-call amount and recipient binding are enforced off-chain by the facilitator.

## How it works (high level)

1. User connects a Solana wallet (Phantom / Solflare / any Standard Wallet).
2. App calls `createSession({ cap, expirationSeconds, ... })` — user signs **one** on-chain `approve` (cap is an encrypted `Euint128`) and **one** Ed25519 auth message.
3. Facilitator submits the `approve` (paying SOL fee), records the session in sqlite, and returns a `sessionId`.
4. App calls `session.fetch("/api/whatever")`. Any `402` is auto-retried with a `PAYMENT-SIGNATURE` header derived from the session — no wallet popup.
5. Resource server forwards the header to the facilitator's `/settle`. Facilitator atomically debits sqlite, builds an `IncoToken::transfer` tx, signs as fee payer, and submits. Encrypted balances are updated inside **Inco Lightning**'s TEE.
